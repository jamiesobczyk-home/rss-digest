Einstein's relativity rules chemical bonds in heavy elements, new research shows

Brown University chemists have provided direct experimental evidence that textbook triple bond structures break down in heavy elements like bismuth, where relativistic effects become significant. Using photoelectron spectroscopy, they found that carbon-bismuth bonds have a hybrid structure of one pi bond and two sigma-pi bonds rather than the traditional one sigma and two pi bonds, because relativistic forces cause electrons to orbit so fast that spin-orbit coupling disrupts the separation between bond types. The findings could prompt a rewriting of chemistry textbooks for how bonding works in heavy elements.
