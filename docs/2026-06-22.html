1983 Northern Telecom Commodore Phone

In the early 1980s, Commodore bundled a specially branded Northern Telecom rotary phone with their VICModem in Canada because Canadian phones were hardwired to their bases, unlike modular phones elsewhere, making the modem unusable in its standard form. Bell Canada, which owned all phones in the country, required the phones be manufactured by its subsidiary Northern Electric and could only be sold as part of the VICModem package. Commodore engineered the VIC-1605 adaptor with a toggle switch to route signals between the phone and modem, making this Canada-only bundle a rare piece of computing history.
