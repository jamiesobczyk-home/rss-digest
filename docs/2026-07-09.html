Anthropic Research · 7:00 PM

Jul 8, 2026AlignmentAn off switch for dual use knowledge in AI models

Anthropic and AE Studio developed GRAM (Gradient-Routed Auxiliary Modules), a method that creates removable compartments within AI models to isolate dual-use knowledge—information usable for both beneficial and harmful purposes—such as virology or cybersecurity. During training, when the model encounters dual-use data, only the dedicated module for that category learns while general-purpose weights are frozen, preventing harmful knowledge from diffusing across the entire network. A single GRAM-trained model can be configured multiple ways after training (16 different versions in their experiments with four dual-use categories), either deleting risky modules entirely or keeping them for trusted deployments, eliminating the need to train separate models.

Shoebox-Sized 'Detector Satellites' Could Sniff Out a Nuclear Bomb In Space

A new study proposes using shoebox-sized detector satellites to identify nuclear weapons in space by sensing distinctive radiation signatures created when nuclear material interacts with particles in Earth's Van Allen radiation belt. Researcher Areg Danagoulian's design uses cubesats equipped with neutron detectors that would approach suspicious satellites and take about a week to confirm a nuclear weapon's presence, or hours with a constellation of 10 satellites. If a nuclear weapon is detected, military could jam its communications to prevent detonation, addressing the current lack of reliable detection methods for space-based nukes.

Hacker News · 7:32 PM

Remote Attestation

Remote attestation uses TPM cryptography to verify that network hosts have expected hardware, firmware, kernel, and filesystem configurations, providing strong guarantees against kernel and driver supply chain attacks. The verified boot state can be enforced by encrypting the root filesystem, refusing certificates to misconfigured hosts, or using TPM-backed TLS authentication to prevent untrusted hosts from operating. Implementing remote attestation organization-wide requires substantial changes to firmware distribution and boot processes, though it provides the security foundation needed for further protections like EDR and confidential computing.
