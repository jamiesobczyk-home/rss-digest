CTOs agree that AI adoption has shifted from unrestricted spending to demanding ROI, though most organizations lack financial models to track token usage and measure actual returns on AI investment. Your brain was never designed for this much bad news

Human brains evolved to detect local threats, but modern news cycles overwhelm this ancient defense system by flooding us with global disasters 24/7, creating what researchers call "news fatigue." The negativity bias that kept our ancestors alive—prioritizing threats over positive information—now works against us as our nervous system struggles to process irrelevant distant dangers before lunchtime. Rather than avoiding news, experts recommend building healthier consumption habits to manage what researchers term "Problematic News Consumption," a pattern that causes preoccupation, dysregulation, and disruption to daily life.

Developers don't understand CORS (2019)

Many web developers misunderstand how CORS works, as demonstrated by Zoom's security vulnerability where they bypassed CORS by encoding data in image dimensions rather than implementing proper CORS headers.
