Sony erases digital content from libraries; reminded we don't own what we buy

Sony is removing 551 previously purchased movies and shows from UK PlayStation customers' libraries starting September 1 due to expired licensing agreements with StudioCanal. The incident underscores that digital purchases are revocable licenses rather than owned content, and Sony has similarly deleted digital libraries in other regions and services. Some customers are demanding refunds and calling for digital stores to stop using the term "purchase" for what are effectively temporary licenses.

Hacker News · 5:48 AM

Parse, Don't Validate – In a Language That Doesn't Want You To

The article advocates for parsing over validation in TypeScript: parsers preserve validation results by encoding them in the type system, while validators discard that information, forcing constant re-checking throughout code. TypeScript's structural typing actively undermines this approach—unlike Haskell or Elm, the language won't enforce type-based guarantees after validation occurs. The solution involves "branding" types to create nominally-typed distinctions that the type system can remember and enforce.

The US ambassador had Belgian police stop our reporting

Journalists from The European Correspondent were detained by Belgian police and removed from a US "Freedom 250" anniversary celebration in Brussels after attempting to ask US Ambassador Bill White about a previous controversy.
