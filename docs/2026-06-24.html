A deadly fungus that can infect cats and people is spreading

A deadly fungus called Sporothrix brasiliensis is spreading through South America, having infected over 11,000 people and thousands of cats since the 1990s, causing painful skin ulcers and potentially fatal infections. The fungus, which primarily affects cats through close contact, has reached Brazil, Paraguay, Chile, Argentina, and Uruguay, and CDC officials warn it's only a matter of time before it reaches the United States, likely through infected pets brought by travelers. The infection can be fatal in both cats and humans without antifungal treatment, and the fungus's unusual ability to survive as a mold in soil before transforming into yeast when infecting living hosts makes it particularly well-suited to spreading globally.

Europe: The World's Fastest-Warming Continent

Europe is warming nearly twice as fast as the global average—2.4°C above preindustrial levels compared to 1.4°C globally—due to land warming faster than oceans, intensified heatwaves from atmospheric circulation shifts, and its proximity to the Arctic, which is heating 3.2°C faster.
